01 / 05

Sort your travel photos

You just got back from Japan and want to move 347 photos into the right folders.

BeforeYou need the exact command. One wrong folder name and photos go missing.
$ mv ~/Downloads/DCIM/*.jpg ~/Pictures/Japan\ Trip/ mv: No such file or directory: Japan Trip ← Spaces in folder names break the command silently.
HotateJust say where you want them.
~move my Japan trip photos to the travel folder Found 347 photos from March 2025 in Downloads... ✓ 347 photos moved to 旅行 / Japan Trip

Before, a space in a folder name was enough to break everything. Hotate handles those details so you don't have to.

02 / 05

Delete photos you don't need

You want to free up space by removing blurry shots from your Beppu onsen trip.

BeforeOne wrong flag and it deletes instantly. No warning, no undo.
$ find ~/Pictures/Beppu -name "*.jpg" -mtime +30 -delete ← What does -mtime +30 mean exactly? ← Already deleted. No confirmation. No undo.
HotateShows you exactly what will be deleted. You choose.
~delete my old Beppu photos
This will remove 89 photos (4.2 GB)
From your Beppu folder · March 12–15, 2025
This cannot be undone

Before, files were gone the moment you pressed enter. Hotate always shows you what will be deleted — and waits for you to say yes.

03 / 05

Open your food blog on your computer

You built a ramen review site and want to see how it looks before sharing it.

BeforeYou need to know what kind of project it is, then remember the right command.
$ cd ramen-blog && npm run dev Error: Cannot find module 'vite' $ npm install added 312 packages... $ npm run dev Local: http://localhost:5173/ ← Three tries just to open a file.
HotateFigures out what your project needs and opens it for you.
~open my ramen blog on my computer Found: ramen-blog — installing 2 missing things... ✓ Your blog is running — tap to open localhost:5173

Before, you'd need to know the difference between npm, yarn, and python just to open a file. Hotate figures that out quietly.

04 / 05

Rename 200 photos at once

You have Fukuoka and Kyoto photos mixed together, all named IMG_0001 to IMG_0200. You want them sorted by city.

BeforeRequires writing a script. Most people just do it by hand for hours.
$ for f in Fukuoka/*.jpg; do mv "$f" "Fukuoka_$f"; done ← No preview of the new names before it runs. ← One mistake and all 200 files are named wrong.
HotateShows the new names before changing anything.
~rename my trip photos by city name
Here's what the new names will look like
IMG_0001.jpg → Fukuoka_IMG_0001.jpg
IMG_0002.jpg → Fukuoka_IMG_0002.jpg
IMG_0089.jpg → Kyoto_IMG_0089.jpg
...and 197 more

Before, batch renaming required knowing how to write scripts. Hotate does it in one sentence, with a full preview before touching anything.

HotateTells you what each thing is and whether it's safe to remove.
